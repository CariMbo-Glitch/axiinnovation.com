The Cost of Losing Patients Along the Journey

Patient non-adherence remains one of healthcare's most expensive blind spots. The American healthcare system loses $100–300 billion annually to medication non-adherence alone—a figure driven not by negligence but by structural barriers that prevent patients from reaching, starting, and staying on therapy.

The Scale of the Problem: Half of all patients fail to take medications as prescribed. Nearly a quarter of prescriptions are never filled. In conditions like hyperlipidemia, the never-fill rate exceeds 50%. For rare disease patients, diagnostic delays average 6+ years, each year compounding costs and clinical damage.

Speed matters clinically. In oncology, treatment delays of just one week increase mortality risk by 1.2–3.2%. When copays exceed $100, patient drop-off hits 75%. Every barrier—missed appointments, prior authorization delays, lack of patient education, supply chain friction—compounds into lost time and lost outcomes.

PatNav maps this entire journey from symptom recognition through diagnosis, treatment initiation, access, and long-term adherence. We identify which barriers drive the biggest delays and which interventions return the highest ROI. Real-world evidence anchors every strategy in observable patient behavior.

How PatNav Works

Barrier & Enhancer Identification

Our analysis isolates which factors delay patients most and which accelerate them. This pinpoints high-ROI intervention sites for your product and patient population.

Intervention Roadmapping

Outcome Measurement
