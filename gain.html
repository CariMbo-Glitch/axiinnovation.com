The Strategic Imperative

The global pharmaceutical market is expanding rapidly—from $1.67 trillion in 2024 to a projected $2.3 trillion by 2030, according to IQVIA. Yet this growth masks a fundamental challenge: 34% of new pharma launches fail to meet year-one sales forecasts, largely due to reimbursement miscalculation and access barriers. Reference pricing mistakes alone can erode more than $20 million annually per market.

Launch sequencing errors create cascading consequences. A misstep in a single market can trigger unintended international reference price reductions across markets 15 times larger. The medtech sector ($640B in 2024, rising to $717B by 2030) and the exploding biotech market (growing at 13% CAGR) face parallel pressures. Generics and biosimilars—representing $415–491B and $22–34B respectively—are competing for increasingly constrained access budgets, with $217–236B in branded drugs losing exclusivity between 2025 and 2030. Implantable devices ($97–133B today, projected to reach $189B by 2030) and prosthetics/orthotics ($6.56B→$8.48B) demand precision navigation across heterogeneous regulatory and pricing regimes.

Time-to-reimbursement variance is dramatic: Switzerland achieves approval-to-reimbursement in 6 months, while the UK requires 17.7 months. Germany averages 7.4 months, the US 9.2 months, and France 12.9 months. Each month of delay compounds revenue loss and market share erosion. Smart access strategy requires real-time visibility into market-by-market reimbursement status, IRP/ERP network effects, and competitive white space.
