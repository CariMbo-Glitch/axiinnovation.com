The Economics of Evidence

In today's competitive pharmaceutical landscape, the difference between rapid market access and extended delays often comes down to the quality, timeliness, and strategic framing of health economic evidence. HTA-Axi was designed to address a fundamental challenge: while global pharma R&D spending reached $288 billion in 2024, the time from regulatory approval to market reimbursement across major markets varies dramatically—from 6 to 17.7 months depending on jurisdiction and evidence readiness.

The economics are compelling: HTA submissions can cost $50,000 to $300,000+ per country, yet over 34% of product launches fail year-one forecasts, with more than half of failures attributed to market access challenges rather than clinical efficacy. For every month delayed in reimbursement, innovative therapies lose millions in potential revenue—making the velocity of evidence synthesis not just a regulatory concern, but a commercial imperative.

HTA-Axi transforms this equation by systematizing the creation of comprehensive, multi-HTA-body-compliant dossiers. We integrate rigorous literature synthesis, cost-effectiveness modeling, budget impact analysis, indirect treatment comparisons, and health outcome evidence into submission-ready documents architected for NICE, G-BA, HAS, CADTH, PBAC, and CONITEC specifications. The result: compressed timelines that preserve patent runway, reduced re-submission risk, and faster payer alignment.
