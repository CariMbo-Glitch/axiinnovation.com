The Industry Reality

The six fractures that are costing pharma and medtech billions in lost opportunity

While top-performing organizations invest in cross-functional intelligence infrastructure, the majority of pharma and medtech companies still operate with fragmented data, siloed teams, and reactive strategies. The consequences are measurable and compounding.

🗒️

Siloed Intelligence, Fragmented Decisions

Access, Commercial, Marketing, and Government Affairs each operate from different data sets, different timelines, and different assumptions. When a pricing decision is made without real-time HTA intelligence, or when a launch sequence ignores patient journey barriers, the result is misalignment at the exact moment coordination matters most.

Only 11% achieve enterprise-wide data integration
⏳

Speed-to-Insight Gap

Traditional consulting engagements deliver landscape analyses in 8–12 weeks. By the time the report lands on your desk, two competitors have already filed submissions, a new HTA recommendation has been published, and the regulatory environment has shifted. The intelligence is accurate but irrelevant.

Avg. 8–12 weeks per landscape report
💰

The Patent Cliff and Pricing Squeeze

With the ongoing patent cliff, increased competition from biosimilars, and cost-motivated regulations like the Inflation Reduction Act in the U.S. and EU pharmaceutical legislation reform, margins are compressing at an unprecedented rate. Companies that lack real-time competitive positioning data cannot defend their value propositions effectively.

Projected 31% revenue decrease from IRA alone through 2039
🚶

The Last-Mile Patient Problem

The challenge has shifted from convincing physicians to prescribe, to identifying patients who need therapy in the first place. Hard-to-diagnose conditions remain buried in fragmented health system data. Without end-to-end patient journey intelligence, commercially viable therapies fail to reach the populations they were designed to serve.

Projected shortage of 10M healthcare workers by 2030
🏛️

Policy Blind Spots

Government affairs teams are operating in an increasingly volatile policy landscape, where healthcare legislation can shift reimbursement frameworks overnight. Without real-time stakeholder intelligence and legislative tracking, organizations are caught reacting to policy changes instead of anticipating and shaping them.

Healthcare policy changes accelerating 3× vs. decade ago
📈

Competitors Who Moved First

The most disruptive threat is not a new molecule — it is a competitor with better intelligence infrastructure. Organizations that have invested in cross-functional intelligence platforms are launching faster, securing reimbursement sooner, and capturing market share before slower-moving companies even complete their internal alignment processes.

Top performers launch 40–60% faster
