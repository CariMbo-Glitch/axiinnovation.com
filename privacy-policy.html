11. Data Anonymization and Pseudonymization

Axi Innovation works with sensitive healthcare and pharmaceutical intelligence. We consider robust anonymization and pseudonymization not merely a regulatory requirement, but a core operating principle. Our approach follows a layered framework that applies the most appropriate technique at each stage of data processing.

11.1. Our anonymization principles

  • Privacy by design and by default: Anonymization is embedded into the architecture of every Axi Innovation solution (GAiN, HTAxi, PatNav, eGov) from the earliest design stage, not applied as an afterthought.
  • Data minimization: We collect and retain only the data strictly necessary to deliver the requested service. Unnecessary identifiers are removed at the point of ingestion.
  • Purpose limitation: Data is processed only for the specific, declared purpose for which it was collected. Once the purpose has been fulfilled, data is anonymized or securely deleted.
  • Continuous risk assessment: We regularly evaluate re-identification risks, particularly in light of the Court of Justice of the EU’s September 2025 ruling that pseudonymized data may still qualify as personal data if a residual risk of re-identification exists.

11.2. Techniques we apply

Technique Description Where applied at Axi Innovation
Irreversible anonymization Personal identifiers are permanently removed or transformed so that no individual can be re-identified, directly or indirectly. Once anonymized, data falls outside the scope of GDPR, LGPD, and CCPA. All aggregated intelligence reports, competitive benchmarking data, and published market analytics.
Pseudonymization Identifiers are replaced with artificial keys (pseudonyms). The mapping table is stored separately under strict access controls. Data remains subject to privacy regulations but carries reduced risk. Internal operational databases, CRM records, and longitudinal client engagement tracking.
k-Anonymity Quasi-identifiers (e.g., country, role, therapeutic area) are generalized so that each record is indistinguishable from at least k−1 other records, preventing singling out. Patient journey analytics (PatNav), where demographic and geographic attributes are generalized before analysis.
Differential privacy Controlled statistical noise is injected into query results so that no individual record materially influences the output, providing mathematically provable privacy guarantees. Aggregated reporting outputs shared externally or published in trend analyses.
Data masking and suppression Sensitive fields (names, contact details) are masked or suppressed in datasets used for secondary analysis, quality assurance, or training. All non-production environments, demonstration datasets, and internal quality reviews.

11.3. Governance and accountability

  • Anonymization review board: All data flows involving personal data are reviewed by our DPO and a designated anonymization specialist before release, ensuring that the chosen technique is appropriate for the context and risk level.
  • Re-identification testing: We periodically conduct re-identification audits on anonymized datasets, using adversarial methodologies, to validate that our anonymization thresholds remain effective as data environments evolve.
  • Third-party obligations: All data processors and subprocessors are contractually required to apply anonymization or pseudonymization standards no less protective than our own.
  • Transparency: Where feasible, we disclose the anonymization technique applied to any dataset shared with clients, enabling them to make informed decisions about downstream use in their own regulatory environments.

Our commitment: Axi Innovation treats anonymization as a continuous discipline, not a one-time event. We invest in advancing our anonymization capabilities as regulatory expectations, re-identification techniques, and data volumes evolve.
